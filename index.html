About me

PhD student in physical chemistry. My research focus lies on synthesis and analysis of perovskite solar cells (see publications below). Recent expertise includes work with AuNP-ZnO compound materials, regarding a plasmonic enhancement for laser sintering (University of Cambridge). Structural control of diblock copolymers for customized mechanical properties of hydrogels (Colorado State University). Extractions from crude oil via hypercritical CO2 and analysis with ESI ICP-MS (Max-Planck-Institute in Mülheim) and synthesis of Sb-based precursors for CVD processes (University of Duisburg-Essen).

For more details about me, have a look at my curriculum vitae.

Publications.

  • Our most recent work is a spectroscopic study of hybrid organic–inorganic MAPbX3 perovskite derivatives (MA = CH3NH3, X = Cl, Br, I). Low-temperature measurements as well as measurements under on- and off-resonance conditions allowed us to unambiguously measure the "true" Raman spectrum. This work is published in the Journal of Physical Chemistry C
